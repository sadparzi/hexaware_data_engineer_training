1. What is DevOps?

Answer:

DevOps is a software development approach that brings the Development (Dev) and Operations (Ops) teams together. Its goal is to automate software development, testing, deployment, and monitoring so applications can be released faster and with fewer errors.

Simple definition:

DevOps is a culture and set of practices that help developers and operations teams work together to deliver software quickly and reliably.


2. Advantages of DevOps
Faster software delivery
Better collaboration between development and operations teams
Continuous Integration and Continuous Deployment (CI/CD)
Reduced manual work through automation
Faster bug detection and fixing
Improved software quality
Higher application reliability
Easier monitoring and maintenance
Quicker recovery from failures
Increased customer satisfaction

3. What is Agile Methodology and What is Scrum?
Agile Methodology

Agile is a software development methodology where work is completed in small, manageable parts called iterations or sprints. Customer feedback is collected regularly, and changes can be made throughout the project.

Simple definition:

Agile is a flexible approach to software development where software is developed, tested, and delivered in small increments.

Scrum

Scrum is one of the most popular Agile frameworks. It organizes work into fixed-length sprints (usually 2–4 weeks), and team members collaborate through regular meetings to deliver working software.

Key Scrum Roles:

Product Owner
Scrum Master
Development Team

4. What are the Important Meetings in Agile?
Sprint Planning
Conducted at the beginning of each sprint.
The team decides which tasks will be completed during the sprint.
Daily Stand-up (Daily Scrum)
A short 15-minute meeting held every day.
Each team member answers:
What did I do yesterday?
What will I do today?
Is there any blocker?
Sprint Review
Held at the end of the sprint.
The completed work is demonstrated to stakeholders and feedback is collected.
Sprint Retrospective
Conducted after the sprint review.
The team discusses:
What went well?
What didn't go well?
What can be improved in the next sprint?
Backlog Refinement (Backlog Grooming)
Product backlog items are reviewed, clarified, and prioritized for future sprints.
